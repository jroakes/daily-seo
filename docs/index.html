Microsoft Faces Potential EU Fine Over Bing AI Risks

Microsoft is facing a potential multi-billion dollar fine from the EU over concerns about misinformation and manipulation risks associated with Bing's AI features, including Copilot. The EU Commission is investigating Bing's AI for safety risks such as deepfakes and manipulation. Microsoft has a deadline of May 27 to respond to the EU's request for information regarding its Bing artificial intelligence services. Failure to comply could result in a fine of up to 1% of its annual revenue.

Digital Decay: 38% of Webpages From 2013 Have Vanished

A Pew Research Center study has revealed widespread digital decay, with 38% of webpages from 2013 no longer accessible. The study also found that 8% of pages from 2023 are already unavailable. Furthermore, 23% of news webpages and 21% of government webpages contain at least one broken link, highlighting the issue of link rot and the importance of content maintenance.

Google AI Overviews in Search Results

Google has been rolling out AI Overviews in its search results, causing concern among searchers as there is no way to turn them off. Bing, on the other hand, allows users to disable AI (Copilot) responses with a click of a button. Google's AI Overviews are AI-generated responses to queries, and early assessments suggest they may impact website traffic. The feature's inclusion in search results is currently limited, appearing in a small percentage of tracked desktop and mobile SERPs in the US.
